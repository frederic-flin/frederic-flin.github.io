Snow and Ice Science

  • Fascination of snow crystals: a very good page on snow crystals created by Yoshinori Furukawa.

  • An attractive site about snow crystals created by Kenneth G. Libbrecht. The site provides also a useful page on ice properties and constants.

  • A site dedicated to snow SEM, with beautiful images.

  • The Snow Research Center web site, which presents current research in the laboratory.

  • The Snow Physics web site of the SLF.

  • The web site of the Snow and Ice Research Group, part of the Japanese National Research Institute for Earth Science and Disaster Prevention.

  • The Cold Region Research and Engineering Laboratory (CRREL), its downloadable technical publications, and the Snow Interest Group (SIG).

  • The Laboratoire de Glaciologie et Geophysique de l'Environnement (LGGE).

  • The last International Classification for Seasonal Snow on the Ground.

Imaging, Digital Geometry and Numerical Modeling

  • David Coeurjolly: algorithmics and digital geometry for curve and surface characterization.

  • Edouard Thiel: chamfer distance geometry (in French).

  • Reinhard Klette: shape recovery, image analysis, computer vision.

  • J. A. Sethian's web site about fast marching methods and level sets methods.

  • The homepage of W. Brent Lindquist: geometry, geostatistics, simulation, front tracking.

  • Edward J. Garboczi's page on structure and properties of cement-based materials: cement hydratation modeling, percolation, finite element/difference methods for digital images.

  • The Laboratoire d'InfoRmatique en Image et Systèmes d'information (LIRIS).

  • The LAboratoire de MAthématiques de l’université de Savoie (LAMA).
